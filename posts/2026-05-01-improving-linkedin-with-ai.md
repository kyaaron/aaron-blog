## A stagnant LinkedIn profile
My LinkedIn profile has remained largely the same for several years. I would get a new job, update the duties and descriptions, and engage with whatever I would see on my news feed. I was and still am pretty good at the engagement part. But my LinkedIn header had been the same for awhile, the job duties were just a list of tasks I was responsible for, and I didn't focus on any keywords. It was an okay LinkedIn profile, but it was nothing special.

A few years ago, I watched [Danny Thompson's LinkedIn series](https://youtu.be/SG5Sb5WTV_g?si=iEX2Iu5CIK48ecJp) on Youtube, which I used to make a few adjustments. I think it's still a good series for people just starting out making their profile, but I was more interested in fine-tuning my specific profile to be higher up on recruiter searches. I also didn't want to post my LinkedIn in a public Discord server or on a public call. 

## An idea to use LLMs
Enter AI. I was having coffee with one of my friends and he suggested posting my public LinkedIn URL into an LLM to see if it could scan my profile and give me a recruiter visibility score of 0-100. He had just finished putting my Github repo list into Chat GPT to see what technologies I had built with (and he said hiring managers would be doing this too to get the low-down on what stacks developers build with). Why not try with LinkedIn?

LinkedIn is a Microsoft product. So that meant Claude was out of the running. It returned an answer stating it could not access my LinkedIn page. That made sense I guess, since Anthropic and Microsoft do not have a partnership that I was aware of. I tried Gemini, which is my usual go-to LLM and CLI tool for development. Gemini was "okay." It couldn't scan my profile but suggested I copy and paste my profile header, my about section, and job duties from my last few recent jobs. It gave me a score of 62/100. Not terrible, but my profile wouldn't be turning any heads in the recruiting circles.

## Making some nice changes
Lastly I pasted my profile link into ChatGPT. I don't use ChatGPT for much anymore, as I prefer Claude and Gemini for any AI-assisted developing I'm doing (mind you, I also hand-write a lot to keep myself in practice). But ChatGPT and Microsoft are on much friendlier terms. It was able to access my LinkedIn URL and profile page, scan everything, and give me a score. It gave me a score of 68/100. Again, not awesome.

I started making some changes it recommended. My header was too bland, so it suggested a new header. I used most of what it suggested but updated a few words to be more accurate.

It also helped me rewrite my job duties. Instead of just a list of duties, it suggested ways to explain the "task-action-result" of everything I did. It also suggested ways to explain my tasks from my non-technical roles in a way that would be interesting for someone hiring software developers. I had never thought of that!

Lastly, it gave me a few tips on improving the About section. I added a techinical skills section to specifically list languages, frameworks, and skills relevant to software development that I was well-versed in. The ending recruiter attention score for my profile after a few hours of work was now an 84/1000. Much better!

## Overall thoughts
I think my LinkedIn profile has improved as a result of using LLMs for review. I found it interesting that different LLMs can or cannot access LinkedIn URLs. I think that's lame and stupid, but that's the way the corporate world works sometimes. I still think having an actual human, especially one who is a current recruiter, reviewing my profile is much more valuable. But I think it's also fair to say an LLM can do a solid job.

I made these changes yesterday. I haven't had a recruiter contact me yet, but I'll keep working on my profile to make it more interesting and attractive. This was a cool learning experience using AI, and it provided some content for this blog as well.
