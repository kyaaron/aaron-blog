I created my Codewars profile just a few months ago in December as part of the curriculum for 100Devs. I was familiar with the site from several years ago, but I had not committed to daily challenges in a long time. For those unaware, Codewars gives coding problems at various levels (called “kyu”) to solve. The idea is you start at the bottom and, through daily practice and review, you get better over time in a specific language. Codewars has multiple languages you can study (they even have COBOL problems!).

Per Leon’s recommendation, I started with the Codewars Fundamentals, which recommends a daily challenge covering fundamentals of the chosen language. I’m pretty sure it uses your profile’s rank to make suggestions on the difficulty level. I started out at the lowest rank which is 8kyu and started solving Javascript problems at that level. These are very basic problems that you can solve after spending a day or two learning the basics of JavaScript.

After about a week of doing 8kyu problems, my profile leveled up to 7kyu and I started working on those problems. These were a bit tougher than the 8kyu problems. I could solve an 8kyu problem almost instantly, but 7kyu problems could take me anywhere from 5–15 minutes to think through. Around this time, I started [pushing my solutions to Github in a Codewars repository](https://github.com/kyaaron/codewars) I created. Anyone can see the progress I make over time, and it helps keep those little code squares green each day.

For about a month I solved only 7kyu problems. I would skip the 8kyu problems. My cadence was solve the problem, then review other solutions to see how other people solved problems. I saw creative solutions using method chaining, switch statements, and built-in JavaScript methods I hadn’t heard of yet (like Array.every()). **Reading other solutions has turned into my favorite part.** It’s a reward for solving the problem. Some answers are funny or insane and would be terrible for production code, but they can be creative. Seeing a complex problem solved in one line with 8 method chains is…a choice. But it’s fun to read through them, understand them, and try to bring some of those methods and solutions to future coding solutions.

I’ve noticed now that I try to use built-in methods like Array.map or Array.forEach more than a simple “for” loop now. Through this practice, I’ve also realized that the “for” loop is still the preferred method for certain situations like iterating through an array up to a certain point but not wanting to go all the way.

A few days ago, my profile leveled up to 6kyu. It was time to advance. I’m three days into solving 6kyu problems. I think the jump from 7 to 6 is much higher than the jump from 8 to 7. These problems are taking me 15–30 minutes to solve. I’ve quickly noticed that I write about 25–40 lines to solve a problem, while others can still do it in 3–10 lines. Some of this is by choice — I’m choosing to be more verbose, declare variables on separate lines, and comment my code. But in other situations, I could stand to use more elegant JavaScript solutions. It’s all part of the learning process.

So that’s where I’m at. Hopefully in a month or two I can move on to 5kyu problems. I’ve seen others from 100Devs reach the 2kyu rank with 6,000+ honor (mine is currently at 137). That’s the goal!
